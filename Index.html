What is Treaty 1?

Treaty 1 was signed in 1871 between the Canadian government and Indigenous peoples: the Anishinaabe, Cree, and Ojibwe. It talked about land, rights, and promises.

Where Was It Signed?

The treaty was signed in what is now Winnipeg, Manitoba. It was a place where many people could meet.

Map of Treaty 1 Land

This map shows the land that is part of Treaty 1, mostly in southern Manitoba.

Who Was Involved?

  • Anishinaabe (Saulteaux) - Lived in southern Manitoba
  • Cree - Lived in northern Manitoba
  • Ojibwe - Lived around Lake Ontario and into Manitoba
